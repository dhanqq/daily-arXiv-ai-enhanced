  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 探索p-新模Hecke特征形式之间的同余关系，特别是模p高幂次的同余现象，这超出了现有p-adic特征形式变化理论所能解释的范围。

Method: 基于显式计算提出猜想，并受全局图景启发，构建描述固定权重、变化L-不变量和相反Atkin-Lehner符号的半稳定表示之间同余关系的局部猜想。

Abstract: We study congruences modulo powers of a prime $p$ between pairs of $p$-new modular Hecke eigenforms of level $Γ_0(p)$ and same weight $k$. This is a new phenomenon that is not explained by the known results on the $p$-adic variation of eigenforms. Inspired by the global picture, we formulate a local conjecture describing congruences between semistable representations of fixed weight, varying $\mathcal L$-invariant, and opposite Atkin--Lehner signs. We give some theoretical evidence towards our conjectures.

### [2] [Szemerédi's Theorem Along Cantor Sets of Integers](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.15299)

Main category: math.NT

TL;DR: 该论文证明了对于具有限制数字的Cantor整数集，存在正下密度集合B使得任意正上Banach密度的整数子集A包含ℓ+1项等差数列，步长为k_n（n∈B）。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 扩展Furstenberg和Katznelson的IP遍历定理以及Kra和Shalom近期工作，研究具有限制数字的Cantor整数集上的遍历性质。

Method: 使用遍历理论方法，分析Cantor整数集（模b具有限制数字的整数集）上的遍历性质，证明相关极限下界为正。

Result: 证明了对于任意正上Banach密度的整数子集A，存在正下Banach密度的集合B，使得A包含ℓ+1项等差数列，步长为k_n（n∈B）。

Conclusion: 该结果是Furstenberg-Katznelson IP遍历定理的推广，也是对Kra-Shalom和Burgin相关工作的补充，建立了具有限制数字的Cantor整数集上的遍历性质。

Abstract: Let $\mathcal C= \{k_1<k_2 < \cdots\}$ be Cantor set of integers, that is a set of integers with restricted digits modulo a base $b$, and suppose $0$ is one of the restricted digits. We show that $$ \liminf_N \Expectation_{n\in [N]} m(A\cap T^{-k_n} A \cap \cdots
  \cap T^{-\ell k_n} A )>0. $$ This is an extension of the IP Ergodic Theorem of Furstenberg and Katznelson, and a partial extension of recent work of Kra and Shalom. In particular, this implies that for any subset of integers $A$ of positive upper Banach density, there is a set $B$ of integers $n$ of positive lower Banach density such that $A$ contains an $\ell+1$ term progression, with step size $k_n$, where $n\in B$. This is a complement to recent results of Kra and Shalom, for IP Sets of integers, and Burgin, concerning Sarkozy's Theorem for Primes with restricted digits.
